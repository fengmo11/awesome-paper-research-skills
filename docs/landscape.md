# Landscape

This landscape groups AI research automation projects by the part of the paper
pipeline they support.

## Full-Cycle Research Agents

These systems attempt to connect several stages: question generation,
literature grounding, experiment execution, analysis, paper drafting, and
review.

- SakanaAI/AI-Scientist
- aiming-lab/AutoResearchClaw
- Technion-Kishony-lab/data-to-paper
- InternScience/InternAgent
- jataware/open-coscientist

Use these when you want to study architecture or build an end-to-end demo. Do
not treat outputs as submission-ready without expert review.

## Writing And Drafting Systems

These projects are strongest after the topic or evidence base already exists.

- NousResearch/hermes-agent
- federicodeponte/opendraft
- Research-Equality/RE-paper-writing
- nanoAgentTeam/research-claw

Use these for outlines, section drafts, long-form synthesis, LaTeX/DOCX export,
and revision loops.

## Literature And Citation Tools

These tools are the best starting point for reliable, narrow projects.

- openags/paper-search-mcp
- jkitchin/litdb
- sypsyp97/AutoCitation
- markrussinovich/refchecker

Good public demo ideas:

- PDF to claim-evidence table.
- arXiv topic tracker.
- Citation metadata verifier.
- BibTeX quality checker.
- Related-work map from a seed paper.

## Review And Quality Tools

These focus on checking, scoring, and summarizing research quality.

- poldrack/ai-peer-review
- tarun7r/deep-research-agent
- mlnjsh/ai-research-radar

Good public demo ideas:

- Review scorecard for a PDF.
- Reproducibility checklist generator.
- Weekly trend report for a field.
- Paper credibility score with source trace.
